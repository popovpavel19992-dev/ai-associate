Problem & Market Opportunity

Small businesses sign contracts they don't understand — and it costs them thousands every year.

The Core Problem

Every small business signs dozens of contracts annually: office leases, vendor agreements, NDAs, employment contracts, SaaS terms, insurance policies. Each one contains clauses that could cost them thousands if they're not caught.

The catch-22: hiring an attorney at $300+/hour for every contract is unaffordable. Not reviewing them is risky. Most small business owners choose to sign and hope for the best.

Why Now?

LLM Breakthrough

Models like Claude can now understand and analyze legal documents with high accuracy. 200K token context windows mean entire contracts fit in a single prompt. This wasn't possible 2 years ago.

Cost Structure Shift

AI analysis costs $0.50-2.00 per document vs. $500+ for an attorney. This 250x cost reduction makes professional-grade analysis accessible to every small business.

Digital-First Business

Post-pandemic, more contracts are digital. Remote work and online business formation have accelerated contract volume for small businesses.

Regulatory Pressure

Growing privacy laws (GDPR, CCPA, state privacy acts) mean more contracts with compliance implications. SMBs need help understanding these obligations.

Chapter 07

Legal Compliance & UPL Protection

The single most important regulatory risk in legal tech — and exactly how ClearTerms handles it.

Understanding UPL

Unauthorized Practice of Law (UPL)

Every US state prohibits non-lawyers from giving legal advice. The definition varies by state but generally includes giving specific advice about someone's particular legal situation. AI tools that say "don't sign this" or "you should negotiate" are potentially violating UPL laws.

✅ Legal Information (Safe)

  • "This clause allows termination with 30-day notice"
  • "This rate is above market average"
  • "This section contains an arbitration clause"
  • "Similar contracts typically include X"
  • "This is flagged because it's unusual for this type"

❌ Legal Advice (UPL Risk)

  • "You should not sign this contract"
  • "I recommend removing section 5"
  • "You have a strong case against the landlord"
  • "Your best option is to negotiate"
  • "You should consult a lawyer about this"

Precedents

DoNotPay Settlement (2024)

Settled class action for $193K for falsely claiming to be a "robot lawyer." Multiple state bars investigated. Key lesson: never position as a legal service provider. Always position as a technology/information tool.
